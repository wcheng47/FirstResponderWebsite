The Problem

Industrial workplaces like manufacturing facilities, distribution centers, and warehouses constantly strive to reduce workplace hazards like burns, chemical spills, falling articles, and heavy equipment accidents. However, as the demand for more efficient and fast-paced workplaces increases, accidents will continue be a factor in industrial work. When accidents happen in large and distributed physical areas, a quick response time and accurate localization is vital to reduce the risk of serious injury and complication.

The Solution

Our solution intends to reduce the response time to workplace accidents and will provide first responders with an accurate location as to where an accident occurs. A high-risk worker (e.g. chemicals technician) will wear a mobile device like a FitBit or Apple Watch that will passively listen for concerning signals like a spike in heart rate or increased galvanic skin response. Upon detecting a concerning spike in these two metrics, the system will instantly notify designated first responders within the company as to the workerâ€™s location, live biomedical signals, location (both latitude-longitude and altitude). The first responder selected can also be the one specifically trained to handle the particular accident. For example, if the incident occurs in an avionics manufacturing lab, the first responder selected can be prepared to address electronics-related injuries like noxious fumes inhalation, electrical shock, or electrical burns.
